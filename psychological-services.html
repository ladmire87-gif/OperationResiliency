Psychological Services & Counseling

Veteran-informed counseling from providers who understand service, transition, trauma, stress, and the weight of carrying responsibility.

Support From Providers Who Understand

Our psychological services are built around respect, trust, and practical support. Placeholder clinic language: many of our providers are veterans or have deep experience working with veterans, which helps them understand military culture, operational stress, transition challenges, and the difficulty many people feel when asking for help. Our goal is to provide care that feels grounded, confidential, and useful — not judgmental or generic.

Services Offered

  • EMDR therapy: Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing for trauma-related symptoms and distressing memories.
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy: Practical work on thoughts, behaviors, habits, stress responses, and coping strategies.
  • Trauma-informed counseling: Support for PTSD symptoms, moral injury, grief, anger, hypervigilance, and adjustment challenges.
  • Anxiety and depression counseling: Care for worry, panic, low mood, isolation, motivation issues, and emotional exhaustion.
  • Relationship and family support: Communication tools, boundary-setting, transition support, and conflict management.

How Counseling Can Help

Counseling gives patients a place to unpack what they have been carrying and build skills that work in real life. For veterans, that may include learning how to quiet the nervous system, improve sleep routines, process trauma, reconnect with family, manage anger, or rebuild identity after service. We meet patients where they are and help them move toward steadier days, healthier relationships, and a stronger sense of control.
